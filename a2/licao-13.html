Opening Dialogue

Manager: "Remember, you have to be on time for the meeting at 8 AM."

Employee: "I know. Do I have to wear a tie today, or is a formal shirt enough?"

Manager: "You don't have to wear a tie, but you have to look professional for the clients."

Employee: "Perfect. Then I can prepare the report now, and I don't have to change my whole outfit."

Grammar: Have to vs. Don't have to

Have to (Obrigação)

Usado para regras externas ou necessidades.

  • "I have to go now."
  • "He has to study."

Don't have to (Falta de necessidade)

Significa que você não precisa fazer algo (é opcional).

  • "You don't have to pay."
  • "She doesn't have to work."
Note: "Don't have to" NÃO é proibição. É apenas "não é necessário".
