Grammar: Past Continuous

Describing Background Actions

We use the Past Continuous to describe an action in progress at a specific time in the past. It sets the scene for a story.

Structure: was/were + verb-ing

  • I / He / She / It was working.
  • We / You / They were playing.
  • He wasn't sleeping.
  • Were they studying?

Past Continuous vs. Simple Past

We often use both together. The Past Continuous describes the longer, background action. The Simple Past describes a shorter, completed action that interrupted it.

I was watching TV (longer action) when the phone rang (short action).

While they were playing soccer, it started to rain.
