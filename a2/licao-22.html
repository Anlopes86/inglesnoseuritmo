Medical consultation

Doctor:

"Hello. What seems to be the problem today?"

Patient:

"I've been feeling short of breath, and I have pain in my chest."

Doctor:

"How long have you felt like this? Does it get worse when you walk?"

Patient:

"Since yesterday afternoon. Yes, a little. I also feel tired and dizzy."

Discuss

1. Which symptoms are common and which are serious?

2. What questions should a doctor ask first in this situation?

3. Is it important to use simple language with patients? Why?

Useful Language for a Consultation

Use estas perguntas para avançar a conversa em uma consulta: localizar a dor, entender a duração e descobrir sintomas extras.

Doctor asks:

Where does it hurt?

How long have you had it?

Does it get worse at night?

Patient answers:

It hurts in my chest.

I've had it since yesterday.

Yes, especially when I walk.

Doctor asks:

Do you have any other symptoms?

Do you have a fever?

Are you taking any medicine?

Patient answers:

I also feel dizzy.

No, but I feel weak.

Yes, just painkillers.

Consultation Builder

Build the patient response

Monte uma resposta completa para o médico com sintoma principal, duração e um detalhe extra.
Meta da atividade: responder em um bloco só, sem travar entre frases curtas. Isso ajuda o aluno a soar mais natural em consulta.

1. Main symptom

2. Time

3. Extra detail

Depois de montar a resposta, pratique a entonação de paciente: sintoma principal primeiro, detalhe extra depois.

Roleplay: Doctor and Patient

Use the prompts to speak:

Student A: Doctor

Ask about the main symptom.

Ask how long the patient has felt like this.

Ask one more follow-up question.

Student B: Patient

You have a fever, nausea, and dizziness.

It started yesterday evening.

You also feel weak after standing up.

Try one round with short answers and another round with fuller answers, so the student learns to expand naturally.
