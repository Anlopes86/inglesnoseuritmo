City vs. countryside

Teacher: "Do you prefer the city or the countryside?"

Student: "I usually prefer the city because it is more exciting."

Teacher: "What makes it more exciting for you?"

Student: "There are more things to do, and public transportation is better."

Teacher: "That makes sense. Is the countryside better in any way?"

Student: "Yes. It is quieter, cleaner, and sometimes safer than the city."

Grammar: Comparative Adjectives

Comparing Two Things

We use comparative adjectives to compare differences between two objects, people, or places.

Short Adjectives (1-2 syllables)

Add -er to the adjective + than.

  • old → older than
  • big → bigger than (double consonant)
  • busy → busier than (y → i + er)

Ex: Brazil is bigger than Portugal.

Long Adjectives (2+ syllables)

Use more + adjective + than.

  • beautiful → more beautiful than
  • expensive → more expensive than

Ex: Paris is more beautiful than London.

Irregular Adjectives

good → better

bad → worse

far → farther / further
