Sharing opinions

Teacher: "Which do you prefer for a pet, dogs or cats?"

Student: "I think dogs are friendlier and more active."

Teacher: "Interesting. In my opinion, cats are easier to take care of."

Student: "That's true, but for me dogs are more fun because they like to play."

Teacher: "So you agree that cats are easier, but you still prefer dogs?"

Student: "Exactly. I understand both sides, but I still prefer dogs."

Grammar: Expressing Opinions

Giving Your Opinion

To express what you think, you can use these phrases:

  • I think pizza is delicious.
  • In my opinion, summer is better than winter.
  • For me, reading is more interesting than watching TV.

Agreeing and Disagreeing

Here's how you can respond to someone's opinion.

Agreeing

  • I agree.
  • I think so too.
  • That's true.

Disagreeing

  • I disagree.
  • I don't think so.
  • I'm not so sure.
