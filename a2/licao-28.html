Opening dialogue

Check in and ask for help

Receptionist:

Good evening. Welcome to the Blue River Hotel. How can I help you?

Guest:

Hi. I have a reservation under Carla Mendes.

Receptionist:

Great. You're in room 304. Here is your key card.

Guest:

Thank you. Could I have another towel, please?

Receptionist:

Of course. I will bring one up right away.

Conversation focus

What the guest needs to handle

Short, polite and clear is the goal in hotel English.

1. Check-in

Say your name and reservation.

Confirm the room or booking type.

Ask where to go next if needed.

Example: I have a reservation under Carla Mendes. Is breakfast included?

2. Request

Use Could I have... for polite help.

Ask for towels, pillows or bags help.

Use one extra detail to sound more natural.

Example: Could I have another towel, please?

3. Problem

Explain the issue in one clear sentence.

Say if something does not work.

Add a simple follow-up request or ask for help.

Example: The air conditioner isn't working. Could you check it?
