Model dialogue

Start, turn, landmark, destination

Visitor:

Excuse me, how do I get to the subway station?

Local:

Go straight for one block, turn left at the bank, and cross the street.

Visitor:

Is it far?

Local:

No, not really. It is next to the bakery.

Visitor:

So I go straight, turn left, cross the street, and the station is next to the bakery?

Local:

Exactly.

Why it works

Imperatives são diretos e naturais para instruções. Ligue etapas com and, then ou after that.

1. Start moving.
2. Change direction.
3. Give the landmark.

Reading: helping a tourist

Tom is visiting a new city. He wants to go to the history museum, but he is lost. He asks a woman for help near the station.

She says, "Go straight for two blocks and turn right at the traffic light. Then walk past the bank. The museum is next to the park, across from a bakery."

Tom repeats the directions to check: "So I go straight, turn right, walk past the bank, and the museum is next to the park?" The woman smiles and says, "That's right."

1. Where does Tom want to go? (dica: o museu de história)

2. Where does Tom ask for help? (dica: perto da estação)

3. What does he do at the traffic light? He . (dica: vira à direita)

4. The museum is next to the . (dica: parque)

5. The museum is across from a . (dica: padaria)
