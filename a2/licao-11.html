Grammar Focus: Superlatives

Short Adjectives

Adicione -est ao final. Use sempre "the" antes.

Fast → The fastest
Old → The oldest
Big* → The biggest
Happy* → The happiest

*CVC (consoante-vogal-consoante) dobra a letra final. Terminado em 'y' vira 'iest'.

Long Adjectives

Use the most antes do adjetivo. O adjetivo não muda.

Modern → The most modern
Beautiful → The most beautiful
Interesting → The most interesting
Expensive → The most expensive

The Difference: Tall vs. High

Tall: Para altura vertical de seres vivos (pessoas, girafas) ou objetos estreitos e altos (prédios, torres).

"He is the tallest man." | "The Eiffel Tower is tall."

High: Para altitude ou distância em relação ao chão. Usado para montanhas, muros, prateleiras ou nuvens.

"Everest is the highest mountain." | "The plane is high."

Irregular Forms (The Exceptions)

  • Good → The best (O melhor)
  • Bad → The worst (O pior)
  • Far → The farthest / furthest (O mais longe)

Task 1: The Short Superlatives

1. Mount Everest is mountain. (use: high)

2. The Sahara is desert. (use: hot)

3. Russia is country. (use: big)

4. He is basketball player. (use: tall)

5. This is bird in the world. (use: small)

6. Antarctica is place. (use: cold)

Task 2: Long & Irregular Superlatives

1. Paris is city. (use: beautiful)

2. That was pizza ever! (use: good)

3. This is car. (use: expensive)

4. It was movie ever. (use: bad)

5. She is singer. (use: popular)

6. Science is subject. (use: interesting)
