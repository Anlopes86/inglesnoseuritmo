World records

Describe places and things using superlatives with confidence.

Nesta aula, o aluno usa superlativos para falar de lugares famosos, prédios, paisagens e experiências marcantes com frases mais completas.

Focus points

  • adjetivos curtos e longos no superlativo;
  • formas irregulares como best e worst;
  • contraste entre tall e high.

Opening Dialogue

Guide:

"Welcome! This is the tallest building in the city, and it attracts visitors from all over the world."

Tourist:

"Wow! Is it also the most expensive hotel in the area?"

Guide:

"No, but it has the best view of the ocean and the most famous rooftop restaurant."

Tourist:

"I understand now. This is the most amazing experience of my trip."

Grammar Focus: Superlatives

Short Adjectives

Adicione -est ao final. Use sempre "the" antes.

Fast → The fastest
Old → The oldest
Big* → The biggest
Happy* → The happiest

*CVC (consoante-vogal-consoante) dobra a letra final. Terminado em 'y' vira 'iest'.

Long Adjectives

Use the most antes do adjetivo. O adjetivo não muda.

Modern → The most modern
Beautiful → The most beautiful
Interesting → The most interesting
Expensive → The most expensive

The Difference: Tall vs. High

Tall: Para altura vertical de seres vivos (pessoas, girafas) ou objetos estreitos e altos (prédios, torres).

"He is the tallest man." | "The Eiffel Tower is tall."

High: Para altitude ou distância em relação ao chão. Usado para montanhas, muros, prateleiras ou nuvens.

"Everest is the highest mountain." | "The plane is high."

Irregular Forms (The Exceptions)

  • Good → The best (O melhor)
  • Bad → The worst (O pior)
  • Far → The farthest / furthest (O mais longe)

Task 1: The Short Superlatives

1. Mount Everest is mountain. (use: alto)

2. The Sahara is desert. (use: quente)

3. Russia is country. (use: grande)

4. He is basketball player. (use: alto)

5. This is bird in the world. (use: pequeno)

6. Antarctica is place. (use: frio)

Task 2: Long & Irregular Superlatives

1. Paris is city. (use: bonito)

2. That was pizza ever! (use: bom)

3. This is car. (use: caro)

4. It was movie ever. (use: pior)

5. She is singer. (use: popular)

6. Science is subject. (use: interessante)

Homework: Your Records

Write 6 superlative sentences about your life:

Try to include places, people, food and one personal opinion. This helps the superlatives sound more natural and less isolated.

  • The best city you know.
  • The most beautiful beach you visited.
  • The oldest person you know.
  • The most delicious meal you've had.
  • The tallest building in your city.
  • The most interesting hobby you have.
