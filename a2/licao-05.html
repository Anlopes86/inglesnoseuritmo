Grammar: More Comparisons

Comparing Equality: (not) as ... as

We use as + adjective + as to say two things are the same. We use not as + adjective + as to say they are different.

  • (+) My car is as fast as your car. (They have the same speed)
  • (-) The book is not as expensive as the game. (The game is more expensive)

Limits: too & enough

We use too to show something is excessive (more than necessary). We use enough to show the correct or sufficient amount.

too + adjective

  • The coffee is too hot to drink.
  • This box is too heavy for me.

adjective + enough

  • He is strong enough to lift it.
  • The water is warm enough to swim.
