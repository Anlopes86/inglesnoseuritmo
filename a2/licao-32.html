Grammar focus

A2 Grammar at a glance

1 · Past Simple vs Present Perfect

Use past simple para ações com tempo definido. Use present perfect para experiências sem tempo definido.

I studied English last year.

✔ Tempo definido: "last year"

I have visited another city.

✔ Experiência de vida, sem tempo

2 · Future: will vs going to

Going to = plano já decidido. Will = decisão espontânea ou promessa.

I am going to practice more.

✔ Plano já feito

I will help you right now.

✔ Decisão no momento

3 · Conditionals

1st conditional (futuro real): if + present → will. 2nd conditional (hipótese): if + past → would.

If I have time, I will review.

✔ Real / provável

If I had more confidence, I would speak more.

✔ Hipotético / sonho

4 · Modals & Comparatives

You should speak every week.

should = conselho

You must review this lesson.

must = obrigação

My answers are clearer now.

comparativo regular

Error correction

Find and fix the mistake

Cada frase tem um erro gramatical típico do A2. Identifique o erro, corrija oralmente e depois clique em "Revelar".

1. I have visited Paris last summer.

Dica: o tempo verbal e "last summer"

✅ I visited Paris last summer. ("last summer" exige past simple)

2. She don't study yesterday.

Dica: auxiliar negativo no passado

✅ She didn't study yesterday.

3. If it will rain, we stay home.

Dica: 1st conditional — estrutura do if

✅ If it rains, we will stay home.

4. He said that he is busy yesterday.

Dica: reported speech — backshift

✅ He said that he was busy.

5. This city is more clean than before.

Dica: comparativo de adjetivos curtos

✅ This city is cleaner than before.

6. I am going to call you — I decide right now.

Dica: decisão espontânea no momento

✅ I will call you. (decisão espontânea → will)

7. Please turn of the computer.

Dica: phrasal verb

✅ Please turn off the computer.

8. If I will have more time, I would study more.

Dica: 2nd conditional — if + ?

✅ If I had more time, I would study more.

Elaborated dialogue

Real conversations

Conversation 1 — Good news

T:

Hey, you look different today. Did something happen?

S:

Yeah, I just got some great news. My company offered me a position in the international team.

T:

Wow, that is amazing! How do you feel about it?

S:

Excited, but a bit nervous too. I mean, I will have to use English every single day now.

T:

That is actually perfect for you. What made them choose you?

S:

My manager said that I had improved a lot this year. She mentioned that my written English was especially good.

Conversation 2 — Still improving

T:

That is great feedback. And what about speaking?

S:

That is still my weak point, honestly. I get nervous when I have to speak in meetings.

T:

I understand. What do you think would help?

S:

If I had more chances to practice in real situations, I think I would feel more confident.

T:

That is exactly what we are going to work on.
