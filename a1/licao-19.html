Grammar: There is / There are

We use "There is" and "There are" to say that something exists.

1. Singular (one thing): There is

(+) There is a bed in the bedroom.

(-) There is not (isn't) a sofa in the kitchen.

(?) Is there a table? -> Yes, there is. / No, there isn't.

2. Plural (two or more things): There are

(+) There are two chairs in the living room.

(-) There are not (aren't) four windows.

(?) Are there two beds? -> Yes, there are. / No, there aren't.

Homework: My Room

Write a short paragraph describing your bedroom or your dream house.

Use "There is" and "There are" and include:

  • At least 3 singular items (e.g., "There is a big bed.")
  • At least 1 plural item (e.g., "There are many books.")
  • At least 1 negative sentence (e.g., "There isn't a TV.")
