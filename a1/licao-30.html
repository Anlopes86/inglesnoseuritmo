A1 - Lesson 30: Future Plans

Warm-up: Yesterday vs. Tomorrow

Let's connect the past and the future.

Professor: "Hello! Let's start by looking back. Did you watch a movie yesterday?" (Review)

Aluno: "Yes, I watched a movie." / "No, I didn't watch a movie."

Professor: "Great! That was yesterday. Now, what about tomorrow? Do you have plans for tomorrow?"

Aluno: "Yes, I will... work."

Professor: "Good! A great way to say this is: 'I am going to work tomorrow.' Today we will learn all about talking about our future plans!"

Grammar: Future with "going to"

Use for plans and intentions.

Structure: Verb to be + going to + Main Verb

(+) I am going to travel next month.

(+) She is going to visit her friend.

(-) He is not (isn't) going to work tomorrow.

(-) They are not (aren't) going to study.

(?) Are you going to watch the movie? -> Yes, I am. / No, I'm not.

(?) What are you going to do tonight?

Homework: Your Resolutions

Write about your plans for next week or next year.

Write at least 3 plans using "going to":

  • Example: "Next week, I am going to study English every day."
  • "I'm not going to watch TV."
  • "My family and I are going to visit my grandmother."
