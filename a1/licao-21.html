A1 - Lesson 21: How much/many?

Warm-up: Your City

Let's talk about quantities.

Professor: "Hello! Last class, we talked about prepositions. Where is the bank in your city?" (Review)

Aluno: "The bank is next to the supermarket."

Professor: "Great! Now, let's think about numbers. Are there banks in your city? One, two, ten?"

Aluno: "Yes, there are many banks."

Professor: "And what about water? Is there water in your city? A river, a lake?"

Aluno: "Yes, there is a river."

Professor: "Perfect! Today we'll learn how to ask about quantity, for things we can count, like banks, and things we can't count, like water."

Grammar: How much? vs. How many?

Let's learn when to use each one.

How many? (+ Countable Nouns)

We use How many for things we can count (friends, books, cars).

(?) How many brothers do you have?

(A) I have two brothers.

How much? (+ Uncountable Nouns)

We use How much for things we cannot count (water, time, money, sugar).

(?) How much water do you drink?

(A) I drink a lot of water.
