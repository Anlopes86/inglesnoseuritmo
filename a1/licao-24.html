A1 - Lesson 24: What are you doing?

Warm-up: Right Now!

Let's talk about this moment.

Professor: "Hi! Let's review. Tell me something you can do." (Review)

Aluno: "I can speak English."

Professor: "Excellent! And that is exactly what you are doing right now. You are speaking English. What about me? What am I doing?"

Aluno: "You are speaking English too."

Professor: "Perfect! And what are we doing together? We are having a class. Today, we'll learn to describe actions happening in this exact moment!"

Grammar: Present Continuous

Use for actions happening right now.

Structure: Verb to be (am/is/are) + Main Verb (-ing)

(+) I am working now.

(+) She is reading a book.

(-) He is not (isn't) sleeping.

(-) They are not (aren't) watching TV.

(?) Are you listening to me? -> Yes, I am. / No, I'm not.

(?) What is he doing? -> He is eating.

Practice: What's Happening?

Complete the sentences using the verbs in parentheses.

1. (You / study / English) -> You English.

2. (I / not / watch TV) -> I TV.

3. (She / eat / a sandwich) -> She a sandwich.

4. (They / play / a game?) -> a game?

5. (He / listen / to music) -> He to music.

Homework: Describe a Picture

Look at a photo with people in it (from a magazine or the internet). Write 3 sentences describing what the people are doing.

Use the Present Continuous:

  • Example: "In the picture, a man is reading a book."
  • "A woman is drinking coffee."
  • "Two children are playing."

Listening Activity Script

Situation 1: "Listen. David has a pen in his hand and a notebook on the table. He is at his desk."
(Answer: He is writing / He is working / He is studying)

Situation 2: "Now, listen about Sarah and Tom. They are in the kitchen. The TV is on. They have a big box of popcorn."
(Answer: They are watching TV / They are eating popcorn)
