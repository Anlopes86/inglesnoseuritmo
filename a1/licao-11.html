Diálogo-modelo

Uma conversa real sobre gostos em casa

Teacher:

Let's talk about free time. Do you like music?

Student:

Yes, I do. I really like pop music, and I listen to it every day.

Teacher:

Nice. Does your brother like pop music too?

Student:

No, he doesn't. He likes rock, and he usually watches sports videos at night.

Teacher:

Interesting. And what about your mother? Does she like music?

Student:

Yes, she does. She likes Brazilian music a lot, but she doesn't like loud rock.

Questions with do and does

Question form

AuxiliarUseExample
doI / you / we / theyDo you like jazz? = Você gosta de jazz?
doeshe / she / itDoes she like sports? = Ela gosta de esportes?

Short answers

Yes, I do.
Sim, eu gosto / sim, eu gosto disso.
No, I don't.
Não, eu não gosto.
Yes, she does.
Sim, ela gosta.
No, he doesn't.
Não, ele não gosta.

Como pensar

Primeiro escolha a pessoa. Se for he / she, use does. Se for I / you / we / they, use do.

Erro comum

Com does, o verbo principal volta à base: Does she like...? e não Does she likes...?
