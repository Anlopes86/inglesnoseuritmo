Warm-up: Now vs. Yesterday

Let's talk about time.

Professor: "Hi! Let's start. Where are you right now?" (Review)

Aluno: "I am at home."

Professor: "Correct. You are at home today. What about yesterday? Think about yesterday. Where were you?"

Aluno: "Yesterday, I... at work."

Professor: "Almost! For the past, we say: 'I was at work.' Today we will learn everything about the past of the verb 'to be'!"

Grammar: Simple Past (To Be)

"Am" and "is" become was. "Are" becomes were.

Was / Wasn't

Use with: I, He, She, It

(+) I was tired yesterday.

(-) She was not (wasn't) at the party.

Were / Weren't

Use with: You, We, They

(+) They were happy.

(-) We were not (weren't) late.

Questions

Was he at home? -> Yes, he was. / No, he wasn't.

Were you born in Brazil? -> Yes, I was.
