Your A1 final project

Chegou a hora de juntar tudo: falar sobre quem você é, sua rotina, suas preferências, seu passado recente e seus planos. O objetivo aqui não é falar “perfeito”, e sim mostrar comunicação funcional, que é exatamente o foco do CEFR A1.

Objetivo: integração do A1
Foco: speaking + writing
Encerramento do módulo

Você vai mostrar que consegue

Se apresentar e falar da própria vida
Usar presente, passado básico e futuro com apoio
Manter uma fala curta com começo, meio e fim

Final dialogue model

A conversa final já mistura tempos e temas

Progressão: aqui o aluno já responde mais de uma informação por turno.

Teacher:

Tell me about yourself. Where are you from and what do you do?

Student:

I am from Brazil and I am a student.

Teacher:

What do you usually do in the morning?

Student:

I usually study and drink coffee in the morning.

Teacher:

What did you do yesterday, and what are you going to do tomorrow?

Student:

Yesterday I worked, and tomorrow I am going to visit my family.

Your final presentation structure

Part 1

Who I am

  • Name
  • Country / city
  • Job or study status

Part 2

My daily life

  • Routine
  • Likes and dislikes
  • Abilities and habits

Part 3

My recent past

  • One thing you did yesterday or last weekend
  • One place you went

Part 4

My plan

  • One future plan with going to
  • One personal goal

Build strong project sentences

Complete os blocos que mais aparecem no projeto final. Isso ajuda muito antes da fala livre.

Final interview prompts

Treine respostas orais mais completas com esses cartões de entrevista.

Write your short profile

Use 6 to 8 sentences

Try to include:

  • Who you are
  • Where you are from
  • What you usually do
  • What you like / don't like
  • What you did recently
  • What you are going to do next
Teacher note: o aluno não precisa usar tudo sem erro. O objetivo é combinar ideias simples com alguma organização.

Self-check before presenting

Language

  • At least one present sentence
  • At least one past sentence
  • At least one future sentence
  • Basic connectors like and / but

Delivery

  • Speak slowly and clearly
  • Pause between ideas
  • Use short sentences if needed
  • Keep going even with small mistakes

What A1 means now

At this point, you can give basic personal information, describe routines, talk about simple past experiences, and mention future plans with support. That is real A1 communication.

You can now

  • Introduce yourself
  • Ask and answer simple questions
  • Talk about life, people and places

Next step

  • Connect ideas more naturally
  • Expand vocabulary
  • Start handling longer conversations in A2
