Warm-up: In Your House

Let's talk about your neighborhood.

Professor: "Good to see you! Think about your living room. Is there a TV in your living room?" (Review)

Aluno: "Yes, there is a TV in my living room."

Professor: "Great. And where is it? Is it on the table?"

Aluno: "Yes, it is on the table."

Professor: "Perfect! Now, what about outside? Is there a park near your house?"

Aluno: "No, there isn't a park."

Professor: "Okay! Today we will learn more words to describe exactly where things are. Let's start!"

Grammar: Prepositions of Place

Let's see how to use them in sentences.

"The bank is next to the supermarket."

"The school is between the park and the bank."

"The car is in front of the house."

"The cat is behind the tree."

"The ball is under the table."

"The park is opposite the bank."

Question:

"Excuse me, where is the bank?"

Homework: My Street

Write a short paragraph describing a street in your city or a room in your house.

Use prepositions of place and include:

  • At least 4 places or objects.
  • At least 3 different prepositions (e.g., "next to", "behind", "under").
  • Example: "On my street, there is a bank. The bank is next to a big park. A car is in front of the bank..."
