Warm-up

Warm-up: Do you like...?

Vamos falar sobre o que você gosta e o que não gosta! Clique em 🔊 para ouvir.

Professor: "Let's play a quick game. I'll name something and you tell me — do you like it or not? Ready? Do you like spiders?"
Aluno: "No! I don't like spiders at all. They are scary!"
Professor: "Ha! I don't like them either. Ok, next one — do you like chocolate?"
Aluno: "Yes, I love chocolate! But I don't like dark chocolate. I think it's too bitter."
Professor: "Interesting! And what about your family? Does anyone in your family like things that you don't like?"
Aluno: "Yes! My brother likes horror movies. I don't like them. I think they are too scary."
Professor: "Perfect! You're already using 'don't' and 'doesn't' naturally. That's exactly our grammar topic today!"
💡 Observe: don't like (I, you, we, they) e doesn't like (he, she, it). É isso que vamos estudar hoje!

Gramática

Grammar: DON'T and DOESN'T

Para frases negativas no Simple Present, usamos don't ou doesn't.

Pronome Negativo (-) Exemplo Ouvir
I / you / we / they don't I don't like spiders.
he / she / it doesn't She doesn't like coffee.

✅ Afirmativo → Negativo

I like cats. → I don't like cats.
She likes jazz. → She doesn't like jazz.

⚠️ Atenção!

Com doesn't, o verbo volta para a forma base:

She doesn't likes → She doesn't like ✓

💡 Dica de memória: don't = do not | doesn't = does not. São apenas contrações!
