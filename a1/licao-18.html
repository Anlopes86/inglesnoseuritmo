A1 - Lesson 18: My Job

Warm-up: Talking About Your Week

Let's start with a conversation!

Professor: "Hi there! How are you doing today? Did you have a good week?"

Aluno: "I'm doing well, thanks! Yes, it was a good week. A little busy."

Professor: "I understand. Do you always have a busy week?"

Aluno: "I usually have busy weeks. I study English, go to the gym, and see my friends."

Professor: "That sounds like a great routine. What about your family? For example, what is your father's routine like?"

Aluno: "My father's routine is very different. He wakes up very early, at 5:30 AM."

Professor: "Wow, 5:30 AM is early! That brings us to our topic today: professions. So, tell me, What does your father do?"

Aluno: "He is an engineer."

Professor: "Perfect! Today, we will learn to talk about jobs. Let's start!"

Grammar Focus: Talking about Jobs

Let's review the structures.

1. Asking about professions:

What do you do? -> I am a student.

What does he do? -> He is a doctor.

What does she do? -> She is an engineer.

2. Affirmative and Negative:

(+) He is a teacher.

(-) He is not (isn't) a teacher. He is a doctor.

3. Yes/No Questions:

Are you a singer? -> Yes, I am. / No, I'm not.

Is she a chef? -> Yes, she is. / No, she isn't.

Homework: A Family Member's Job

Choose a family member (father, mother, brother, etc.) and write a short paragraph about their job.

Be sure to include:

  • Their name and relation to you (e.g., "My father, John...")
  • Their profession (e.g., "He is a chef.")
  • Where they work (e.g., "He works in a big restaurant.")
  • Two activities they do at work (e.g., "He cooks pasta and makes salads.")
