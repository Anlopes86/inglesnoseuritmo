A1 - Lesson 29: Questions in the Past

Warm-up: Affirmative vs. Negative

Let's check what you did.

Professor: "Hello! Last class, we talked about yesterday. Tell me one thing you did yesterday." (Review)

Aluno: "I watched a movie."

Professor: "Great! And what about studying? Did you study?"

Aluno: "No. I... not studied."

Professor: "Good try! To make a negative sentence in the past, we say: 'I did not (didn't) study'. And to ask a question, I say: 'Did you study?'. Let's learn this today!"

Grammar: Past Simple (Questions & Negatives)

We use the auxiliary verb DID for questions and negatives.

Negative Sentences

Structure: Subject + did not (didn't) + Verb (base form)

I didn't work yesterday. (NOT: I didn't worked)

She didn't study for the test.

Questions

Structure: Did + Subject + Verb (base form)?

Did you watch the movie? -> Yes, I did. / No, I didn't.

Did he visit his family? -> Yes, he did. / No, he didn't.
