Warm-up: Your Talents

Let's talk about what you do.

Professor: "Hi! In our last class, we used 'a few' and 'a little'. Do you have a little free time today?" (Review)

Aluno: "Yes, I have a little free time."

Professor: "Great! What do you do in your free time? Do you play the guitar?"

Aluno: "No, I don't play the guitar."

Professor: "Okay. So, for 'play the guitar' your ability is no. In English, you say: 'I can't play the guitar'. What about cooking? Is that a 'yes' or 'no' ability for you?"

Aluno: "Cooking is a yes."

Professor: "Excellent! So you say: 'I can cook'. Today we will explore everything we can do!"

Grammar: Using "Can"

"Can" is a modal verb. It's special because it doesn't change!

1. For Ability (Habilidade)

(+) I can speak English.

(-) He cannot (can't) swim.

(?) Can they cook? -> Yes, they can. / No, they can't.

2. For Permission & Requests (Permissão e Pedidos)

(Permission) Can I open the window?

(Request) Can you help me, please?

Practice: Make Sentences

Complete the sentences with can or can't.

1. A fish swim, but it fly.

2. "Excuse me, you tell me the time?"

3. He has a driver's license, so he drive.

4. "I'm cold. close the door?"

Homework: What Can You Do?

Write about your abilities. What are some things you can and can't do?

Write at least 4 sentences:

  • Two sentences with "I can..."
  • Two sentences with "I can't..."
  • Example: "I can cook, but I can't sing."

Whether you think you can, or you think you can't...

...you're right.
