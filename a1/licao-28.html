A1 - Lesson 28: What Did You Do Yesterday?

Warm-up: Your Yesterday

Let's talk about the past again.

Professor: "Hello! Last class, we learned about the past of 'to be'. Where were you yesterday morning?" (Review)

Aluno: "I was at home."

Professor: "Great! And at home, what did you do? Did you watch TV? Did you work?"

Aluno: "I worked."

Professor: "Excellent! The verb is 'work' and in the past, it's 'worked'. Today we will learn how to talk about past actions using regular verbs!"

Vocabulary: Regular Verbs in the Past

These verbs end in "-ed" in the past. Click the cards!

Grammar: Simple Past (Regular Verbs)

For regular verbs in the affirmative, we just add -ed.

Structure: Subject + Verb-ed

I worked yesterday.

You studied last night.

He watched a movie.

She listened to music.

We played video games.

They visited their family.

Important!

The verb form is the same for all subjects (I, you, he, she, it, we, they)!
