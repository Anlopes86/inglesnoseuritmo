Grammar — Used To

1. Used To — Past habits and routines

Use used to for actions that happened regularly in the past but don't happen anymore. It expresses a contrast between past and present.

Subject + used to + verb (base form)
TypeExampleMeaning
✅ AffirmativeI used to play soccer every weekend.I did this regularly; I don't anymore
❌ NegativeShe didn't use to like vegetables.She disliked them in the past; maybe she likes them now
❓ QuestionDid you use to live in London?Did this in the past; don't anymore
⏰ Common contexts for Used To: childhood habits · past routines · old hobbies · past fears · former relationships · previous jobs · old behaviors

2. Didn't use to vs didn't — difference

Didn't use to = a habit that is no longer true. Didn't (past simple) = a one-time event.

StructureExampleMeaning
Used ToI didn't use to drink coffee.It was a habit I had; now I drink it
Past SimpleI didn't drink coffee yesterday.One specific moment in the past
Used ToShe used to be my best friend.Was; the relationship changed
Past SimpleShe was my partner on that project.Just a fact about the past

3. Used To vs Present Perfect — comparison

Used To (past habit) Present Perfect (recent change)
I used to smoke a lot. (I don't anymore.) I have stopped smoking. (Recent/relevant to now.)
He used to work as a teacher. He has been a developer for 5 years.
They didn't use to exercise. They have started going to the gym.
Used To = only past habits Present Perfect = past action connected to now

4. Be used to vs Used to — don't confuse!

Be used to = something is normal/familiar to you now. Used to = a past habit.

StructureExamplePronunciation
Be used toI am used to cold weather.YOOST (rhymes with "cooked")
Used toI used to live in Canada.YOOS-tuh (two syllables)
Remember: "Be used to" uses the Present Tense. "Used to" is only for past habits.

Expressions & Phrasal Verbs — Change & Habits

Expression
to grow out of something
Deixar de fazer/gostar de algo quando fica mais velho; superar.
"I grew out of my fear of needles by the time I was a teenager."
Expression
to be stuck in a rut
Estar preso em uma rotina entediante; não conseguir sair de um padrão.
"After 10 years doing the same job, I felt like I was stuck in a rut."
Phrasal Verb
to break out of (something)
Sair/escapar de; livrar-se de uma situação ruim.
"She finally broke out of her shyness and made new friends."
Expression
to go through a phase
Passar por uma fase; experiência temporária.
"As a teenager, I went through a rebellious phase."
Phrasal Verb
to settle down
Acalmar-se; estabelecer-se em um lugar; começar uma vida estável.
"After traveling for years, he finally settled down and got married."
Expression
to come a long way
Progredir muito; fazer grandes avanços.
"You've come a long way since you started learning English!"
Phrasal Verb
to pick up (a hobby/habit)
Começar a fazer algo novo; adquirir um hábito.
"I picked up painting as a hobby during lockdown."

Reading

How I Changed Over the Years

When I look back at photographs from my childhood, I barely recognize the person staring back at me. It's not just about my appearance — it's about who I was on the inside.

As a kid, I used to be extremely afraid of the dark. I wouldn't go anywhere without my parents, and I would cry if they left me with a babysitter. I didn't use to have any friends because I was too shy to talk to other children. School was a nightmare for me.

In high school, things started to change. I used to spend hours alone in my room reading books, avoiding social situations whenever possible. I didn't use to believe in myself, and I definitely didn't think I could achieve anything significant.

But university was a turning point. I had to give presentations, work on group projects, and live away from home. At first, I hated it. However, I realized that the more I pushed myself out of my comfort zone, the stronger I became. I grew out of my fears gradually.

Now, at 28 years old, I'm completely different. I travel alone, I speak in public regularly, and I have a wonderful group of friends. I'm used to challenges now, and I actually seek them out. I've come a long way from that fearful child who wouldn't leave her mother's side.

What's interesting is that I didn't use to value these changes. I thought success was about having money or a prestigious job. Now I understand that the real success is becoming confident in who you are and breaking out of the limitations you place on yourself.
