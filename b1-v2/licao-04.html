Grammar — Present Perfect Continuous

1. Present Perfect Continuous — ongoing actions

Use for actions that started in the past and continue up to now. Focus on the activity itself.

Subject + have/has + been + verb (-ing)
TypeExampleMeaning
✅ AffirmativeI've been studying English for two years.Started 2 years ago, still studying
❌ NegativeShe hasn't been working here very long.Started recently, still working
❓ QuestionHave you been waiting long?How long have you been in this state?

2. Present Perfect vs Present Perfect Continuous

TenseExampleFocus
Present PerfectI have written three books.Completed actions (result matters)
Present Perfect ContinuousI have been writing for hours.Ongoing activity (duration matters)
Present PerfectShe has lived in London since 2019.State (how long in that state)
Present Perfect ContinuousShe has been living in London for 5 years.Activity (continuous experience)

3. Common uses — Present Perfect Continuous

Use for:
• Duration of ongoing activities: "How long have you been learning guitar?"
• Recent activity with visible results: "You've been crying! What's wrong?"
• Repeated actions: "I've been trying to call you all day."
• Ongoing states: "She's been sick for a week."

4. How long have you been...?

QuestionAnswer
How long have you been working here?For three years. / Since 2021.
How long has she been living abroad?For 5 months. / Since January.
Have you been waiting long?No, just 10 minutes.

Reading

Long-Distance Friendship

My best friend moved to another country five years ago, but we've been staying in touch regularly. We've been having weekly video calls every Friday night without fail.

I've been thinking about how strong our friendship is despite the distance. We've been supporting each other through so many changes. When I got a new job, she celebrated with me. When she was heartbroken, I was there to listen.

We've been making plans to visit each other twice a year. This year, we've been planning a big trip together. We've been saving money and coordinating schedules for months!

What makes this friendship special is that we've been growing together, not apart. We've been sharing our dreams, fears, and achievements. We've been bonding over new interests and laughing at inside jokes that only we understand.

I've realized that distance doesn't matter when you've been building a real connection. We've been proving that true friendship can survive anything. I'm grateful for someone who has been there for me, even from thousands of miles away.

Comprehension Questions

Question 1
How long has the author's best friend lived abroad?
For five years.
Question 2
What have they been doing every Friday night?
Having weekly video calls.
Question 3
How many times a year have they been planning to visit each other?
Twice a year.
Question 4
Find three examples of Present Perfect Continuous in the text.
"we've been staying in touch", "we've been having weekly video calls", "we've been supporting each other"
