Grammar — Past Simple vs Present Perfect

1. Past Simple — a finished action

Use when the action is completed and you know when it happened.

Subject + verb (past form) + time expression
TypeExampleTime marker
✅ AffirmativeShe moved to London in 2020.in 2020
❌ NegativeHe didn't study English at school.at school
❓ QuestionDid you make a big decision last year?last year
⏰ Common time markers for Past Simple: yesterday · last week/month/year · in 2019 · two years ago · when I was a child · on my birthday

2. Present Perfect — connection to the present

Use when the exact time is not important, or the action connects to now.

Subject + have/has + past participle
TypeExampleNotes
✅ AffirmativeI have made some great friends.result matters now
❌ NegativeShe hasn't finished school yet.still unfinished
❓ QuestionHave you ever lived abroad?life experience
⏰ Common time markers for Present Perfect: already · yet · ever · never · just · recently · so far · this year · since / for

3. Side-by-side comparison

Past Simple Present Perfect
I lived in Paris for two years. (I left.) I have lived in Paris for two years. (I still live there.)
She saw that movie last week. She has seen that movie. (at some point)
Did you eat yet? (informal US) Have you eaten yet? (standard)
Past Simple = specific time, finished Present Perfect = connected to now

Expressions & Phrasal Verbs

Expression
a turning point
Um momento decisivo que muda a direção da sua vida.
"Losing that job was actually a turning point for me — it pushed me to start my own business."
Expression
to take the plunge
Tomar uma decisão corajosa e difícil depois de hesitar.
"I finally took the plunge and moved to another country."
Phrasal Verb
to give up
Desistir de algo.
"She never gave up, even when things got difficult."
Phrasal Verb
to grow up
Crescer, tornar-se adulto. Também usado para indicar maturidade.
"I grew up in a small town, but I've always dreamed of living in a big city."
Expression
to be a life-changing experience
Uma experiência que transforma profundamente a sua vida.
"Studying abroad was a life-changing experience for me."
Phrasal Verb
to end up (doing something)
Acabar fazendo algo — muitas vezes diferente do planejado.
"I wanted to be a doctor, but I ended up working in technology."
Expression
it's now or never
É agora ou nunca — expressão usada para encorajar uma decisão.
"I thought: it's now or never, so I applied for the job."

Reading

A Letter to My Younger Self

Dear 18-year-old me,

I want to tell you something important: the decisions you are afraid to make right now will become the most valuable lessons of your life. I know you have already chosen to study engineering, but you have always dreamed of becoming a writer. Let me tell you what happened.

In 2015, when I was 24, I attended a creative writing workshop on a whim. I signed up at the last minute, half-expecting to drop out. But something incredible happened — I discovered that I had a voice, and that people wanted to hear it. That single afternoon changed everything.

Since then, I have written three books. The first one took five years to finish. I gave up at least a dozen times. I threw chapters in the bin, started over, and cried at my desk more than once. But I never stopped completely, because deep down I knew: this was my defining moment.

I also want to tell you about failure. I sent my first book to forty-two publishers. Forty-two of them said no. The forty-third said yes. What I learned from those forty-two rejections is more than I ever learned from any of my successes.

So please, don't give up on the things that matter to you. Take the plunge. And remember — it is never too late to become who you were meant to be.

— Your future self, age 35

Comprehension Questions

Question 1
What did the writer always dream of becoming, even though they studied engineering?
They always dreamed of becoming a writer.
Question 2
What happened at the creative writing workshop in 2015?
The writer discovered they had a voice — that people wanted to hear what they had to say. That afternoon changed everything.
Question 3
How many times did publishers reject the first book? What happened in the end?
Forty-two publishers said no. The forty-third said yes.
Question 4
Find two examples of Past Simple and two examples of Present Perfect in the text.
Past Simple: "I attended a workshop", "I signed up", "it took five years", "I sent my first book". Present Perfect: "I have written three books", "I have always dreamed", "I have never stopped completely", "you have already chosen".
