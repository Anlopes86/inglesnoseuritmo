Grammar — Present Perfect: Since / For

1. For vs Since — duration

FOR = length of time (how long). SINCE = starting point (when it began).

I have + verb (past participle) + FOR [duration]
I have + verb (past participle) + SINCE [specific time]
StructureExampleMeaning
FOR (duration)I've lived here for 10 years.Total length of time
SINCE (starting point)I've lived here since 2015.When it began
FOR (duration)She's worked here for three months.3 months total
SINCE (starting point)She's worked here since January.Started in January

2. For — common durations

Time expressions with FOR:
for a week · for two months · for 3 years · for a long time · for ages · for years · for decades · for my whole life

3. Since — common expressions

Time expressions with SINCE:
since 2020 · since last year · since Monday · since childhood · since I was a kid · since I moved · since the beginning · since we met

4. How long have you...?

QuestionAnswer (For)Answer (Since)
How long have you lived here?For 10 years.Since 2015.
How long have you known her?For ages!Since high school.
How long has he been a teacher?For 5 years.Since 2018.

Expressions & Phrasal Verbs — Journeys

Expression
a long way

"He's come a long way since he started."
Phrasal Verb
to get through
Superar, lidar com algo difícil.
"How did you get through that difficult period?"
Expression
milestone
Momento importante ou evento marcante.
"Graduating was a huge milestone for me."
Phrasal Verb
to carry on
Continuar; seguir em frente.
"Despite the setback, she carried on."
Expression
life experience
Experiência de vida; aprendizado acumulado.
"With 20 years of life experience, she's very wise."

Reading

A Life Well-Lived

I've been thinking about my life lately, and it's amazing how much I've accomplished in just 30 years. When I look back, I can see how far I've come since I was a child.

I've been studying languages since I was 10 years old. For the past 20 years, I've learned English, Spanish, and German. It has been one of my greatest achievements.

My career started when I was 22. Since then, I've worked for three different companies, and I've grown so much professionally. I've been at my current job for 5 years, and it's been the most rewarding so far.

I've been married for 3 years now. My spouse and I have been together since university, so we've known each other for over 10 years! We've shared so many experiences together.

What I've learned is that life is a journey, not a destination. Every milestone has brought me closer to understanding who I am. I haven't reached my final goal yet, but I'm enjoying every step of the way. For now, I'm grateful for all the moments that have shaped me into the person I am today.

Comprehension Questions

Question 1
How long has the author been studying languages?
For 20 years. They started when they were 10 years old.
Question 2

For 5 years.
Question 3
Since when have they known their spouse?
Since university. They've known each other for over 10 years.
Question 4
Find three examples of "for" and three examples of "since" in the text.
For: "For 20 years", "for 3 years", "For the past 20 years". Since: "since I was a child", "since I was 10 years old", "since university".
