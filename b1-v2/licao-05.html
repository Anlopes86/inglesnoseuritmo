Grammar — Mixed Tenses Review (Unit 1 Summary)

1. Past Simple — Single events with specific time

Use: One-time events, completed actions, specific past moments
Example: "I graduated in 2020." "We met last year."

2. Used To — Past habits (no longer true)

Use: Repeated past actions that stopped, contrast past vs now
Example: "I used to play soccer every day." "He didn't use to like coffee."

3. Present Perfect — Past connection to now

Use: Experiences, recent actions, unspecified time, life achievements
Example: "I've visited 10 countries." "She has never been late."

4. Present Perfect: Since/For — Duration

Use: How long something has been happening
Example: "I've lived here for 5 years." "She's worked here since 2019."

5. Present Perfect Continuous — Ongoing activity

Use: Actions that started in past and continue now, duration of activity
Example: "I've been studying English for two years." "We've been planning a trip."

6. Quick comparison table

TenseExampleFocus
Past SimpleI studied French in school.Finished action, specific time
Used ToI used to study French.Repeated past habit (stopped)
Present PerfectI have studied French.Unspecified past, connected to now
Pres. Perf. (Since/For)I've studied French for 3 years.Duration (how long)
Pres. Perf. ContinuousI've been studying French.Ongoing activity, duration

Practice Activities — Mixed Tenses

Part A — Identify the correct tense
Question 1
When I was young, I (want) to be an astronaut. (querida ser)
used to want (past habit - no longer true)
Question 2
I (study) engineering since 2021. (desde 2021)
have been studying (Present Perfect Continuous - duration)
Question 3
She (graduate) five years ago. (formou-se)
graduated (Past Simple - specific time in past)
Question 4
They (achieve) their first goal. (alcançaram)
have achieved (Present Perfect - achievement, unspecified time)
Part B — Complete the story
Question 5
My dream: "I (want) to be a teacher. I (always / love) helping others. I (study) education for three years, and I (already / complete) my degree. Next year, I (start) my first job!" (Preencha os 5 espaços)
have wanted / have always loved / have been studying / have already completed / will start
Part C — Error correction
Question 6
Find the error: "Since I was a kid, I've wanted to be a doctor. I used to hope that one day this dream will come true." (Há um erro de tempo verbal)
Error: "will come true" should be "would come true" (past wish, past tense needed after used to)
Question 7
Find the error: "I studied French for five years now and I have never been to France." (Há um erro)
Error: "studied" should be "have been studying" (ongoing action from past to now, use Present Perfect Continuous)

Expressions & Phrasal Verbs — Ambitions

Expression
to follow your dreams
Perseguir seus sonhos; realizar seus objetivos.
"I'm following my dream of becoming a writer."
Phrasal Verb
to work towards
Trabalhar com o objetivo de; esforçar-se para alcançar.
"I've been working towards my promotion for months."
Expression
to never give up
Nunca desistir; perseverar.
"Even when it's hard, I never give up on my goals."
Phrasal Verb
to achieve a goal
Alcançar um objetivo; realizar uma meta.
"I finally achieved my goal of finishing the marathon!"
Expression
to make a difference
Fazer diferença; causar impacto positivo.
"I want to make a difference in the world."
Phrasal Verb
to bring about change
Causar mudança; promover transformação.
"Education brings about positive change in society."
Expression
to have big plans
Ter grandes planos; objetivos ambiciosos.
"She has big plans for her future career."

Reading — Success Stories

From Dreaming to Achieving

I used to work in an office job that I didn't enjoy. I spent eight hours a day wishing I was doing something else. For years, I had a dream: to open my own business. But I never took action.

Everything changed when I turned 30. I realized that I've been waiting too long. I've wasted so much time on excuses. That's when I decided to make my dream a reality.

Since that moment, my life has been completely different. I've been taking business courses for the last two years. I've learned about marketing, finance, and customer service. I've made friendships with other entrepreneurs who share my passion.

Three months ago, I officially launched my business. I've already served hundreds of customers, and I've received wonderful feedback. It hasn't always been easy — there have been challenging days when I wanted to give up. But I've never stopped believing in myself.

Looking back at my journey, I realize that the past defines us only if we allow it. I used to doubt myself, but I've grown so much. I've achieved my dream, and I'm just getting started. My advice to anyone who has a big goal: don't wait. Start today. Your dream is worth pursuing.
