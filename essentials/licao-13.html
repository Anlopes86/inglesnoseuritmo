Lesson 17: Life Experiences

Present Perfect: The Bridge

Imagine que sua vida é um livro de aventuras. O Present Perfect é usado para listar o que você já fez (experiências), sem dizer exatamente o dia ou a hora.

A Regra de Ouro:

Se você diz QUANDO (yesterday, in 2010), use o Simple Past.
Se você foca na EXPERIÊNCIA, use o Present Perfect.

Present Perfect:

"I have visited France." (Já fui, em algum momento da vida).

Simple Past:

"I visited France last year." (Tempo específico).

The Anatomy of Present Perfect

Subject + HAVE / HAS + PAST PARTICIPLE

Auxiliary (Ter)

I / You / We / They → HAVE

He / She / It → HAS

The Verb (3ª Coluna)

Regular: Add -ED (Work → Worked)

Irregular: Muda tudo! (Go → went (passado simples)/gone (passado particípio)

Reading: The Bucket List

Mark is a traveler. He has been to five continents. He has climbed Mount Kilimanjaro and he has flown in a hot air balloon over Turkey. His sister, Anna, has driven a Ferrari in Italy, but she has never seen the ocean. Together, they have had many amazing experiences around the world.

1. How many continents has Mark visited?

2. Has Anna seen the ocean?

3. What has Anna driven in Italy?
