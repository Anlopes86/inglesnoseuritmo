Lesson 12: Possessives & 's

Warm-up: Whose Is It?

You see these objects on a table: a phone, a backpack, a notebook, and keys.

Try to say sentences like: "This is my phone." or "Those keys are hers."

Possessive adjective
my, your, his, her, our, their
Possessive pronoun
mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs

Presentation: Two Ways to Show Possession

1. Possessive adjectives

They come before a noun.

  • My notebook is new.
  • Her bag is black.
  • Their teacher is friendly.

2. Possessive pronouns

They replace the noun.

  • This notebook is mine.
  • The black bag is hers.
  • The teacher is theirs.

Apostrophe s: We also use 's to show possession with names or people.

This is Maria's laptop. These are the students' books.

Practice 1: Choose the Best Option

1. Julia forgot ___ umbrella at school.

2. That red bike is not mine. It is ___.

3. We are in ___ classroom now.

Practice 2: Complete the Sentences

1. This is Ana's book. It is .

2. Those are my parents. That car is .

3. I forgot my jacket, so I borrowed coat.

4. Pedro and Luisa cleaned room yesterday.

Production

Write 4 sentences about possession.

  • Use one possessive adjective.
  • Use one possessive pronoun.
  • Use one sentence with 's.
  • Use one sentence with a plural owner like students'.
