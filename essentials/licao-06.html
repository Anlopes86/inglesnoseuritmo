Presentation: Future with "Going To"

Usamos "Going To" para planos e intenções que já decidimos antes de falar.

Estrutura: Sujeito + am/is/are + going to + Verbo

  • I bought the tickets. I am going to travel to London.
  • She has an important test. She is going to study all weekend.
  • We decided. We are going to watch the new Marvel movie.

Presentation: Future with "Will"

Usamos "Will" para decisões espontâneas, previsões e promessas.

Estrutura: Sujeito + will ('ll) / will not (won't) + Verbo

  • Decisão Espontânea: The phone is ringing. I'll answer it!
  • Previsão: I think it will rain tomorrow.
  • Promessa: Don't worry, I won't tell anyone your secret.
  • Oferta: That bag looks heavy. I'll help you.

Grammar Focus: "Going To" vs "Will"

GOING TO

Planos & Intenções

Usado para uma ação que você já decidiu no passado e planeja executar no futuro.

Exemplo:

"I have my ticket. I am going to fly to Paris tomorrow."

WILL

Decisões Espontâneas

Usado para uma decisão que você toma no momento em que está falando.

Exemplo:

"Paris sounds nice! Maybe I will go there one day."

Practice 1: Choose the Correct Form

Complete as frases com a forma correta: "will" ou "be going to".

1. A: I'm so thirsty. B: Wait, I (get) you a glass of water.

2. We have an exam next week, so we (study) hard.

3. Look at the dark clouds! It (rain).

4. I think the yellow team (win) the game.

5. My brother (start) his new job on Monday.

Practice 2: Plan vs. Decision

Leia a situação e crie uma frase para o futuro.

O que você diz a um amigo? (Use "going to")

O que você decide fazer e diz em voz alta? (Use "will")

Practice 3: Find the Mistake

Encontre o erro em cada frase e reescreva-a corretamente.

1. He is going to travels next month.

2. I bought the tickets. I will fly to Italy.

3. She will helps you with your homework.

4. They going to visit us soon.

Speaking: Your Future
