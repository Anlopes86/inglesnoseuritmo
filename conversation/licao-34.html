Warm-up: True or Suspicious?

Let's talk about hidden agendas, rumors, and strange beliefs.

Why are people attracted to conspiracy theories?

Is it healthy to question the media and official narratives?

Why do some people believe far-fetched ideas so strongly?

Where is the line between critical thinking and paranoia?

Key Expressions

Conspiracy theory

A belief that events are secretly manipulated by powerful groups.

Cover-up

An attempt to hide the truth about a mistake, crime, or secret.

To question everything

To doubt official explanations and examine all possibilities.

Far-fetched

Very unlikely or difficult to believe.

Urban legend

A widely shared story that sounds true but is usually false.

To spread a rumor

To pass unverified information from person to person.

Why People Believe Strange Things

Conspiracies often grow in uncertain times.

When people feel powerless, confused, or distrustful, simple explanations can feel comforting even when the evidence is weak.

Why they spread: fear, identity, distrust, and social media algorithms.
Why they matter: they can damage trust, science, politics, and real people.

Is believing a conspiracy always irrational, or sometimes understandable?
