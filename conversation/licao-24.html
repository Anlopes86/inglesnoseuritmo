Lesson 24: The Gig Economy & Remote Work

Warm-up: Working Hard

Let's talk about work:

If you didn't have to work for money, how would you spend your time?

Do you work to live, or do you live to work?

How many hours a day do you think is "healthy" for a person to work?

Does hard work always lead to success?

Work Vocabulary

To commute

To travel some distance between one's home and place of work regularly.

Burnout

State of emotional, physical, and mental exhaustion caused by excessive stress.

Side Hustle

A secondary job or business to earn extra income.

Overtime

Time worked in addition to one's normal working hours.

To quit

To leave a permanent job or a career.

Workaholic

A person who works compulsively and has trouble relaxing.

Discussion: The Great Resignation

Beyoncé sings "I just quit my job, I'm gonna find new drive." What would make you quit a job today? Is it just about the money or your "soul"?

The lyrics mention working "off past five" and it "working her nerves". How do you deal with a job that affects your sleep and mental health?

In your opinion, what is a "new foundation" for a successful career in 2025?

Discussion: The Reality Gap

The lyrics mention "Running in place just to stay in the race." Do you feel that no matter how hard you work, the cost of living (rent, prices) always catches up?

"The dream that's built on my routine." Is it fair that the "Boss man" lives the dream while the worker "breaks his back"?

"They make the rules, we make it stand." How would the world change if the "working men/women" decided to stop for one week?

Compare the three songs: Beyoncé wants to quit, BTO is taking care of business, and Cain Walker is just surviving. Which one represents your country's current reality better?
