Warm-up: The Spark of Genius

Let's explore the thin line between brilliant innovation and complete madness. What makes an idea 'great'?

What is the most useless invention you have ever seen or heard of?

Do you think the best inventions come from careful planning or by complete accident?

If you could invent a machine to do one boring task for you every day, what would it be?

Have you ever had a 'crazy' idea that failed miserably but was fun to try?

Key Expressions

To reinvent the wheel

To waste time creating something that already exists and works well.
"We don't need a new way to organize files; let's not reinvent the wheel."

A lightbulb moment

A moment of sudden inspiration, revelation, or recognition.
"I was in the shower when I had a lightbulb moment for my new business idea."

Back to the drawing board

To start over because a previous attempt has failed.
"The prototype didn't work at all, so it's back to the drawing board for us."

To dream up an idea

To invent an imaginative or unusual idea.
"To solve this problem, we really need to think outside the box and try something new."

To break new ground

To do something innovative that has not been done before.
"The invention of the smartphone was a complete game changer for the entire world."

To get the hang of it

To learn how to do or use something that is initially difficult.
"The new software is a bit complicated, but you'll get the hang of it after a few days."

Brilliance or Madness?

The inventor's dilemma.

Every great invention was once considered a 'crazy idea' by someone. The difference is often just a matter of timing.

The Genius: Sees a problem that nobody else sees and creates a solution that nobody else imagined.
The Madman: Spends years creating something that solves a problem that doesn't actually exist.

Can you tell the difference before the invention is finished?

The Pitch

Choose ONE question and answer it in 2-3 minutes. Present your crazy ideas and evaluate the inventions of the past.

If you had an unlimited budget, what 'crazy' invention would you try to build?

What is one invention that you think the world would actually be better WITHOUT?

Would you be willing to be the first person to test a dangerous new invention (like a teleporter)?

If you could bring one inventor from the past (like Da Vinci or Tesla) to the year 2026, what would they be most surprised by?

"If at first the idea is not absurd, then there is no hope for it."

Homework

Choose one of the options below and write a short pitch or description (100-150 words).

Option 1

Pitch a new gadget that solves a common daily problem (e.g., an umbrella that never gets lost).

Option 2

Write about an invention from history that you think is completely useless and explain why.

Option 3

Describe how a specific invention from the past (like the lightbulb or the internet) completely changed your life.

✓ Use at least 3 expressions from today's lesson.

✓ Give your invention a catchy name.

✓ Explain why your idea is better than what already exists.
