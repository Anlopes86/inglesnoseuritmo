Key Expressions

To lead by example

To influence through actions, not just words.
"A good manager should always lead by example."

To earn trust

To gain confidence through consistent behavior.
"It takes time to earn trust, but only seconds to lose it."

To delegate effectively

To assign responsibilities clearly and strategically.
"Knowing how to delegate effectively is a key leadership skill."

Accountability

Taking responsibility for decisions and outcomes.
"True leaders accept accountability for their team's failures."

To motivate a team

To inspire people to perform well together.
"She knows exactly how to motivate a team during tough times."

To call the shots

To make the final decisions.
"In this project, the director is the one who calls the shots."

Theme in Context

Leadership is not just a title. It is repeated behavior that makes people trust a direction.

Influence can be healthy or manipulative depending on transparency, responsibility, and whether people feel respected. In this lesson, students should compare authority, charisma, competence, and accountability.

In teams: trust grows when promises and actions match.
In school/work: delegation fails when expectations are vague.
In society: charisma can inspire people or hide weak judgment.
In conflict: accountable leaders admit mistakes before blaming others.
