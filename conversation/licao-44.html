Theme in Context

Leadership is not just a title. It is repeated behavior that makes people trust a direction.

Influence can be healthy or manipulative depending on transparency, responsibility, and whether people feel respected. In this lesson, students should compare authority, charisma, competence, and accountability.

In teams: trust grows when promises and actions match.
In school/work: delegation fails when expectations are vague.
In society: charisma can inspire people or hide weak judgment.
In conflict: accountable leaders admit mistakes before blaming others.
