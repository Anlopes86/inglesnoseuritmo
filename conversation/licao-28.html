Ethical Vocabulary

Dilemma

A situation in which a difficult choice has to be made between two or more alternatives.

Integrity

The quality of being honest and having strong moral principles.

Altruism

The practice of objective and selfless concern for the well-being of others.

Accountable

Required or expected to justify actions or decisions; responsible.

Virtue

Behavior showing high moral standards.

Whistleblower

A person who informs on a person or organization engaged in an illicit activity.

Practice: Moral Dilemmas

The Trolley Problem

A classic ethical dilemma:

A train is out of control. It is headed toward 5 people. You can pull a lever to switch it to another track where there is only 1 person. Do you pull it?

"I pull it to save more lives (Utilitarianism)."
"I don't pull it; I won't choose who lives or dies."

Homework

Write about a time you faced a dilemma and how your integrity guided your decision.

Example: "I once found a phone. It was a dilemma because I was in a hurry, but my virtue led me to wait and return it to the owner."
