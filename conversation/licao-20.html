Warm-up: About Habits

Let's start our conversation:

What are some good habits you'd like to develop?

Is it easier to build a new habit or break an old one?

How do you use technology to track your habits?

Do you believe in the "10,000-hour rule" for mastery?

Key Vocabulary & Expressions

Click the cards to explore ideas about habits.

To stick to a routine

To continue to follow a regular pattern of activities.

A creature of habit

Someone who prefers to do things in a fixed, routine way.

To get into the swing of things

To become accustomed to a new situation or routine.

To fall off the wagon

To return to an old, bad habit after trying to stop.

A vicious cycle

A sequence of bad habits that reinforce each other.

Old habits die hard

It's very difficult to stop doing things you have been doing for a long time.

Homework

Write a short paragraph about a positive habit you'd like to build this month. Explain the steps you will take to achieve it.

Example: "This month, I want to develop the habit of reading for 20 minutes every morning. I will start by placing a book on my nightstand so it's the first thing I see. I'll also use an app to track my progress and stay motivated."
