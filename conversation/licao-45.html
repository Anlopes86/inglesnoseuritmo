Warm-up: The Cost of Victory

Let's discuss competition, ethics, and success.

Is winning the most important thing in a competition?

How do you define 'playing fair'?

Are there situations where bending the rules is acceptable?

How does society treat losers compared to winners?

Key Expressions

To play by the rules

To act fairly and follow regulations.
"If you want to be respected, you have to play by the rules."

A level playing field

A situation where everyone has an equal chance of succeeding.
"We need to create a level playing field for all competitors."

To bend the rules

To interpret rules loosely without technically breaking them.
"Sometimes you have to bend the rules to get things done."

To win at all costs

To do whatever is necessary to achieve victory, regardless of ethics.
"His 'win at all costs' mentality made him unpopular."

A sore loser

Someone who gets angry or upset when they lose.
"Nobody likes playing with a sore loser."

To throw in the towel

To give up or surrender.
"After three failed attempts, he finally threw in the towel."

Ethics of Competition

Winning is celebrated, but how we win matters.

In sports, business, and life, the desire to succeed can sometimes push people to compromise their values.

Fair Play: Winning only counts if the rules were respected.
The Real World: The rules are often bent by those at the top.

Is a victory achieved by bending the rules still a victory?
