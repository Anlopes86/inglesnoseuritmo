Warm-up: Too Many Options

Let's talk about freedom, indecision, and modern decision-making.

Do more options really make people happier?

When does choice become stressful?

Why do people regret decisions even after choosing carefully?

Is "good enough" sometimes better than "perfect"?

Key Expressions

Choice overload

Stress caused by having too many options.

To weigh pros and cons

To compare advantages and disadvantages.

To second-guess

To doubt a decision after making it.

Decision fatigue

Mental exhaustion from many decisions.

To narrow it down

To reduce many options to a few.

Good enough

Acceptable even if not perfect.

Theme in Context

Too many options do not only affect shopping choices. They shape identity, relationships, and long-term goals.

When everything feels possible, commitment feels risky. People delay decisions, compare themselves constantly, and confuse curiosity with direction.

In career: keeping every door open can delay real growth.
In relationships: fear of "missing out" can prevent emotional commitment.
In daily life: small repeated choices can drain energy before important decisions.
In mindset: clarity often comes after commitment, not before it.

Food for Thought

Think about it: choosing one path always means giving up others. The challenge is deciding what kind of regret you can live with.

1. Is it better to make an imperfect decision now or a perfect decision too late?

2. In your life today, where do you need more courage: choosing or committing?

3. What matters more to you right now: keeping options open or building depth in one direction?

Homework

Option 1 - Personal Story: Describe a real decision you delayed for too long. Explain what made it hard, what finally moved you, and what you learned.
Option 2 - Social Analysis: Argue whether modern platforms (streaming, dating apps, social media, shopping apps) increase freedom or anxiety. Use concrete examples.
Option 3 - Philosophy in Practice: Defend one rule for better decisions (for example: "choose values over options"). Explain how this rule would change daily life.
