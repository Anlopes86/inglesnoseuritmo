LESSON 33: THE INVISIBLE LANGUAGE

60:00

IT'S NOT WHAT YOU SAY.
IT'S HOW YOU SAY IT.

Research shows that 55% of a message is delivered through body language.

First Impression:

"You only have 7 seconds to make a first impression. Are your arms crossed? Are you smiling?"

WARM-UP DEBATE:

  • Can you lie in English?
  • How do you show you are listening?

THE ESSENTIAL 6 GESTURES

STEEPLING
Joining fingertips like a "church steeple". Shows confidence and power.
MIRRORING
Subtly copying the other person's posture. Creates rapport and trust.
FIDGETING
Playing with rings, hair, or pens. A classic sign of nervousness or boredom.
EYE BLOCK
Closing eyes or rubbing them. Subconscious attempt to "not see" something unpleasant.
PACIFYING
Touching the neck or collarbone to calm the nervous system.
OPEN PALMS
Showing your palms. An ancient sign of "I have no weapons" / Honesty.

GAME: 2 TRUTHS & 1 LIE

THE RULES:

  • 1. Tell 3 personal stories (2 true, 1 fake).
  • 2. The listener must watch your EYES and HANDS.
  • 3. Guess which one is the LIE.
TEACHER TIP: Force the student to keep their hands visible on the camera to practice "Honesty Cues".
DISCUSSION: "Was it harder to lie because I was watching your body language?"
