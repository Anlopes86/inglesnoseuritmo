Song 1 Debate

Carolina Drama - gossip, truth, and responsibility

Personal

Have you ever heard a story about yourself that wasn't true? How did it feel and what did you do?

Reflection

Why do small communities spread stories faster than big cities? Is it about closeness or boredom?

Opinion

Who is more guilty: the person who invents gossip or the person who repeats it?

Debate

Can a false story ever be more interesting than the truth? Should we care?

Theme in Context

Storytelling is how people organize memory, emotion, and persuasion.

Every story is shaped by the teller. Even true stories change based on who's listening, where you are, and what you want to prove.

In conversation: details reveal character and emotion, not just facts.
In gossip: stories grow with each telling, creating new "truths."
In persuasion: the way you frame a story is more powerful than the story itself.
In identity: the stories people tell about you become part of who you are.

Quick Reflection

Is the storyteller's intention (honest or manipulative) more important than the listener's reaction?

Song 2 Debate

One Bourbon, One Scotch, One Beer - character through what we order

Personal

What "bar routine" do people in your country have? What does someone's order reveal about them?

Reflection

Why is repetition in storytelling funny? When does repetition become annoying instead?

Opinion

Is this character admirable (confident in what he wants) or sad (stuck in a rut)?

Debate

Can a story be funny AND tragic at the same time? Is that more powerful?

Reflection: Character Through Detail

What small detail could tell us everything about a person?

Example: Someone always carries a photograph. Someone else checks their phone every 30 seconds. Someone makes the same joke twice.

A good storyteller reveals character through what people do, not what they say.

Question for you: What habit of yours would tell someone a true story about who you are?
