Theme in Context

Storytelling is how people organize memory, emotion, and persuasion.

A strong story does more than describe events. It chooses a point of view, builds tension, gives the listener a reason to care, and leaves space for interpretation.

In conversation: details make experiences vivid instead of generic.
In persuasion: stories can clarify values faster than statistics alone.
In media: narrative framing can inform people or manipulate them.
In identity: the stories we repeat about ourselves shape future choices.
