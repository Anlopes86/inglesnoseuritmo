Key Expressions

To step out of your comfort zone

To do something that makes you feel uncomfortable in order to grow.
"You need to step out of your comfort zone if you want to improve."

To take a leap of faith

To do something even though you are not sure of the outcome.
"Quitting his job to start a business was a huge leap of faith."

To play it safe

To avoid taking risks.
"I could have invested the money, but I decided to play it safe."

To hold yourself back

To prevent yourself from achieving something due to fear or doubt.
"Don't let fear hold you back from pursuing your dreams."

To face your fears

To confront the things that scare you.
"She finally faced her fears and learned how to swim."

A stepping stone

An action or event that helps you make progress towards a goal.
"This entry-level job is just a stepping stone to a better career."

The Growth Zone

Growth happens at the edge of your comfort zone.

Psychologists often talk about three zones: Comfort, Panic, and Growth.

Comfort Zone: Safe, familiar, but no learning happens here.
Growth Zone: Challenging, slightly scary, where skills are built.
Panic Zone: Too much stress, overwhelming, causes retreat.

How do you know when you are in the Growth Zone versus the Panic Zone?
