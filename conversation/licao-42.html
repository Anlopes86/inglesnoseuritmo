Theme in Context

Biohacking turns health, identity, and ambition into ethical questions.

A conversation about enhancement is never only about technology. It is about consent, inequality, access, pressure, and what we still want to call natural. Students should practice separating curiosity from endorsement and risk from fear.

In health: self-tracking can empower patients, but obsession can damage well-being.
In society: expensive enhancements may widen the gap between rich and poor.
In sports/work: performance pressure can make optional upgrades feel mandatory.
In ethics: consent matters only when people truly understand long-term risks.
