Warm-up: Upgrading Humanity

Let's discuss the intersection of biology, technology, and ethics.

Would you ever consider getting a chip implanted in your hand to pay for things?

Is it ethical to use technology to increase human intelligence?

If science could make you live for 200 years, would you want that?

Where should we draw the line between 'healing' and 'enhancing' the human body?

Key Expressions

To push the boundaries

To test or extend the limits of what is possible.
"Biohackers are pushing the boundaries of what the human body can do."

A double-edged sword

Something that has both favorable and unfavorable consequences.
"Genetic engineering is a double-edged sword; it can cure diseases but also create inequality."

To play God

To act as if one has unlimited power or control over life and death.
"Critics argue that scientists are playing God when they edit human embryos."

The cutting edge

The most advanced or innovative stage of development.
"This lab is at the cutting edge of neural interface research."

To keep in check

To keep something under control or within limits.
"We need strict regulations to keep the development of AI in check."

A slippery slope

A course of action that seems small but leads to a major, often bad, result.
"Some fear that allowing minor genetic tweaks is a slippery slope toward 'designer babies'."

The Post-Human Future

Evolution is now in our hands.

For millions of years, evolution was a slow, natural process. Now, through biohacking and CRISPR, we can edit ourselves.

The Optimist: We can eliminate suffering, disease, and aging. We can become more than human.
The Skeptic: We are destroying our nature and creating a dystopian world of inequality and loss of soul.

Is being 'human' defined by our limitations or our potential?
