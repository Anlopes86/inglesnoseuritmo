Warm-up: Let's Talk

Think about these questions:

Have you ever rebelled against something?

What does “freedom” mean to you?

Do you think young people today are more or less rebellious?

Key Vocabulary & Expressions

Click the cards to explore the ideas of this lesson.

To rebel against something

To fight against authority or rules.

To stand up for your beliefs

To defend what you think is right.

A sense of freedom

The feeling of being free to do what you want.

To break the rules

To do something that is not allowed.

To question authority

To challenge the people or systems in power.

To go against the grain

To do things differently from what is usual or expected.

To fight for a cause

To work hard to achieve or defend a principle.

To have a voice

To have the power to express your opinion and be heard.

Discussion: We’re Not Gonna Take It

Personal

What situations make people feel they have had enough and need to speak up?

Reflection

Is rebellion usually a sign of courage, immaturity, or frustration?

Opinion

What are some things people protest against in schools, families, or society?

Debate

When is it healthy to say "I'm not gonna take it anymore," and when can it become destructive?

Discussion: Fight for Your Right

Personal

What "rights" do teenagers and young adults often have to negotiate at home?

Reflection

Is humor a good way to criticize rules and authority, or can it make the message weaker?

Opinion

Which school or family rules do you think are fair, and which ones feel unnecessary?

Debate

Do young people need more freedom first, or more responsibility first?

Discussion: Born to Be Wild

Feeling

What kind of freedom does this song suggest: emotional freedom, physical freedom, or freedom from rules?

Reflection

Why do so many people connect adventure with freedom and identity?

Opinion

Is adventure an essential part of life, or can a calm routine also feel deeply free?

Debate

Are some people truly "born to be wild," or is that identity something culture teaches us?
