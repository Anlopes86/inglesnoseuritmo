Key Expressions

To be on a winning streak

To win several times in a row.
"The team has been on a winning streak for the past five games."

To give it your all

To make a maximum effort in something.
"Even though they were losing, the players gave it their all until the final whistle."

To level the playing field

To make a situation fair for everyone.
"The new rules were designed to level the playing field for smaller teams."

A nail-biting finish

An extremely tense and exciting ending.
"The match had a nail-biting finish, with the winning goal scored in extra time."

To be a good sport

To accept winning or losing gracefully.
"Even after the defeat, she was a good sport and congratulated the winner."

To raise the stakes

To increase the importance or risk of something.
"Playing in the World Cup final really raises the stakes for every player."

Rivalry: Passion or Poison?

Great rivalries define eras in sport.

From Brazil vs. Argentina to Real Madrid vs. Barcelona, rivalries push athletes to their limits and captivate billions of fans worldwide.

The positive side: Rivalries raise the level of competition, create iconic moments, and inspire entire generations.
The dark side: Rivalries can fuel hatred, violence in stadiums, and toxic nationalism.

Can you admire a rival without hating them?
