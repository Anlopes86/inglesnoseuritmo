Key Expressions

To learn the hard way

To learn something through a painful or difficult experience.

To dwell on

To think about something for too long, especially negatively.

To make up for

To compensate for a mistake or loss by doing something positive.

To own up to

To admit that you did something wrong.

Wish I had

An expression used to talk about regret in the past.

To live and learn

A phrase used to accept mistakes as part of life.

Regret or Growth?

Regret can be heavy, but also clarifying.

Looking back can trap us in guilt, or help us become more honest, careful, and compassionate the next time we face a similar situation.

Unhelpful regret: shame, paralysis, self-hatred.
Helpful regret: insight, accountability, wiser choices.

Do we suffer more from bad decisions, or from unfinished emotional business?
