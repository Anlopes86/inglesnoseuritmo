Key Expressions

To save room for dessert

To avoid eating too much so you can enjoy dessert.
"I really have a sweet tooth; I can't resist chocolate cake."

A piece of cake

Something that is very easy to do.
"Don't worry about the exam; it's going to be a piece of cake."

To take with a grain of salt

To not completely believe something that you are told.
"You should take his stories with a grain of salt; he tends to exaggerate."

Food for thought

Something that warrants serious consideration.
"The documentary gave us a lot of food for thought about the environment."

To be your bread and butter

A person's main source of income.
"Teaching is my bread and butter, but I also do some freelance writing."

To bite off more than you can chew

To try to do something that is too difficult for you.
"I think I bit off more than I could chew by taking on three projects at once."

Food as Identity

You are what you eat — but also where, when, and with whom you eat.

Food is more than fuel: recipes preserve memories, migrations, beliefs, and family history.

Family

Which dish represents your family? Who taught the recipe, and when do you usually eat it?

Migration

When people move, recipes change. Is an adapted dish less authentic, or is adaptation part of its story?

Beliefs

How do religion, ethics, or health choices influence what people consider acceptable food?

Power

Who gets to call a cuisine “exotic,” “traditional,” or “fine dining”? What do those labels reveal?

Mini challenge

Describe one meaningful dish without naming it. Your partner must guess the dish and its cultural connection.
