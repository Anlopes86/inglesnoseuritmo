Talking about the past

Use the Simple Past to talk about finished actions and tell stories.

Nesta aula você aprende a usar o Simple Past com verbos regulares e irregulares para falar sobre o que aconteceu ontem, no fim de semana ou em qualquer momento no passado.

By the end, you can:

  • formar frases afirmativas, negativas e interrogativas no Simple Past;
  • usar os principais verbos irregulares: go → went, see → saw, eat → ate;
  • contar o que fez no fim de semana com naturalidade.

Opening Dialogue — Monday morning at work

Ana:

"Hey! How was your weekend? Did you do anything fun?"

Tom:

"Yes! I went to the beach on Saturday. The weather was perfect. We swam in the sea and ate at a great restaurant."

Ana:

"That sounds amazing! Did you go with your family?"

Tom:

"No, I didn't go with my family. I went with some friends. We had a great time!"

Ana:

"Lucky you! I stayed home and watched movies all weekend."

Grammar Focus: Simple Past

Afirmativa

Sujeito + verbo no passado. Verbos regulares recebem -ed. Irregulares têm forma própria — igual para todas as pessoas.

I / You / He / She / We / They
+ worked / went / ate
"She went to the beach."

Negativa

Use didn't (did not) + verbo no infinitivo. O verbo principal volta para a forma base — nunca no passado!

I / You / He / She / We / They
+ didn't + verb (base)
"She didn't go to work."

Interrogativa

Comece com Did + sujeito + verbo base. Resposta curta: Yes, I did / No, I didn't.

Did+ sujeito + verb (base)?
"Did you eat breakfast?"

Atenção!

❌ "She didn't went home."

✅ "She didn't go home."

Após did / didn't, o verbo sempre fica no infinitivo (forma base), nunca no passado.

Expressões & Phrasal Verbs

Expressões naturais para contar o que aconteceu.

Phrasal Verb

End up

Significado: acabar fazendo algo que não era o plano

"I planned to go out, but I ended up staying home."

Eu planejei sair, mas acabei ficando em casa.

Phrasal Verb

Catch up with

Significado: colocar o papo em dia, reencontrar alguém

"I caught up with some old friends last weekend."

Pus o papo em dia com velhos amigos no fim de semana.

Expressão

Had a blast

Significado: se divertiu muito, curtiu demais

"We went to the concert and had a blast!"

Fomos ao show e nos divertimos muito!

Expressão

Out of nowhere

Significado: do nada, de repente, sem aviso

"Out of nowhere, it started to rain heavily."

Do nada, começou a chover muito.

Leitura: A Weekend to Remember

Um relato de fim de semana usando Simple Past e as expressões da aula.

Texto

Last Saturday, my friend Leo and I decided to do something different. We woke up early, packed our bags, and took the bus to a small town about two hours away. We didn't plan much — we just wanted to explore.

When we arrived, we walked around the old town centre and visited a beautiful church. Then, out of nowhere, it started to rain. We ran into a small café and ended up staying there for two hours. We drank hot chocolate, ate homemade cake, and caught up on everything in our lives.

By the time the rain stopped, we felt completely relaxed. We took a lot of photos and went back home happy. We really had a blast. It was one of the best weekends I can remember.

Perguntas de interpretação

1. O que Leo e o narrador decidiram fazer no sábado?

2. O que aconteceu de repente quando eles estavam na cidade?

3. O que eles fizeram no café?

4. Como eles se sentiram no final do dia?
