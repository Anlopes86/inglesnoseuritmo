Grammar: The Simple Past

Using the Simple Past

We use the Simple Past to talk about completed actions, experiences, and sequences in the past. In A2, the goal is not only to name one action, but to connect events into a short story.

Sentence Structure

For all verbs, the structure for negatives and questions is the same. This is the key contrast of the lesson: affirmative past form versus base verb after did.

Form Structure Example
(+)Subject + Verb in PastShe worked. / She went.
(-)Subject + didn't + Verb (base)She didn't work. / She didn't go.
(?)Did + Subject + Verb (base)?Did she work? / Did she go?

Homework: tell your weekend story

Prepare a short weekend story for the next class. Write it first, but be ready to say it aloud.

Include in your homework

  • Write 8 to 10 lines about your last weekend
  • Include at least 5 different past verbs
  • Use 1 negative sentence and 1 question-answer idea
  • Organize the story with time markers: on Saturday, later, at night, on Sunday
  • Finish with an opinion: It was relaxing / busy / exciting
