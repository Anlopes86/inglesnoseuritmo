Telling a story

Use sequence connectors to tell stories in the right order.

Nesta aula você aprende a usar conectores de sequência — first, then, after that, finally — para contar histórias de forma clara e natural no Simple Past.

By the end, you can:

  • usar first, then, after that, later e finally para ordenar eventos;
  • combinar conectores com o Simple Past para contar uma história completa;
  • usar suddenly e in the end para dar dinamismo à narrativa.

Opening Dialogue — Two friends catching up

Carla:

"You look tired! What happened yesterday?"

Mark:

"Oh, it was a crazy day! First, I missed the bus. Then, I had to walk to work in the rain."

Carla:

"That's terrible! What happened next?"

Mark:

"After that, I arrived late and my boss was angry. Later, I spilled coffee on my laptop. Suddenly, everything went dark — the power went out!"

Carla:

"No way! So what did you do?"

Mark:

"In the end, I just went home and ordered pizza. Best decision of the day!"

Grammar Focus: Sequence Connectors

O que são conectores de sequência?

São palavras ou expressões que indicam a ordem dos eventos em uma história. Eles deixam a narrativa mais clara e natural — como pontes entre as frases.

Ordem dos eventos

FirstPrimeiro (início da história)
ThenEntão, depois
After thatDepois disso
LaterMais tarde
FinallyFinalmente (conclusão)

Expressões extras

SuddenlyDe repente (surpresa)
In the endNo final (resultado)
At firstA princípio, no começo
EventuallyCom o tempo, eventualmente

Como usar na frase

Os conectores geralmente vêm no início da frase, seguidos de vírgula:

"First, I woke up late. Then, I missed the bus. After that, I arrived at work soaking wet."

Finally vs. In the end

❌ "Finally, I decided to go home." (quando quer dizer "no final das contas")

✅ "In the end, I decided to go home."

Finally = último passo de uma sequência. In the end = resultado geral, conclusão de uma situação.

Expressões & Phrasal Verbs

Expressões para tornar sua história mais viva e natural.

Phrasal Verb

Run into

Significado: encontrar alguém por acaso, sem planejar

"I ran into my old teacher at the supermarket."

Encontrei meu antigo professor no supermercado por acaso.

Phrasal Verb

Turn out

Significado: acabar sendo, resultar em algo inesperado

"It turned out to be a great day!"

Acabou sendo um ótimo dia!

Expressão

You won't believe it

Significado: você não vai acreditar (para introduzir algo surpreendente)

"You won't believe it — I won the lottery!"

Você não vai acreditar — ganhei na loteria!

Expressão

To make matters worse

Significado: para piorar ainda mais a situação

"To make matters worse, I forgot my umbrella."

Para piorar ainda mais, esqueci meu guarda-chuva.

Mini Diálogos

Situação 1 — Um dia horrível

A:

"How was your morning?"

B:

"Terrible! First, I woke up late. Then, I couldn't find my keys."

A:

"And then what?"

B:

"After that, I missed the bus. To make matters worse, it started to rain!"

Situação 2 — Uma surpresa boa

A:

"You look happy! What happened?"

B:

"You won't believe it! I ran into my old friend at the café."

A:

"Really? What did you do?"

B:

"We sat and talked for hours. It turned out to be a wonderful afternoon!"

Situação 3 — Contando uma aventura

A:

"Tell me about your trip!"

B:

"First, we took the wrong train. Then, we got lost in the city."

A:

"Oh no! How did it end?"

B:

"In the end, we found a great restaurant and had an amazing dinner!"

Situação 4 — Uma história engraçada

A:

"What's the funniest thing that happened to you recently?"

B:

"Yesterday, I rushed to work, but suddenly I realized I was wearing two different shoes!"

A:

"Ha! What did you do?"

B:

"I went home and changed. After that, I was very late — but at least I matched!"

Leitura: The Day Everything Went Wrong

Uma história em sequência usando os conectores da aula.

Texto

Last Monday was one of those days when nothing goes right. First, my alarm didn't ring, so I woke up an hour late. I jumped out of bed and got dressed in five minutes. Then, I ran to the kitchen, but there was no milk for my coffee. I grabbed a banana and rushed out the door.

After that, I ran to the bus stop, but the bus had already left. To make matters worse, it started to rain heavily. I called a taxi, but it took twenty minutes to arrive. Later, when I finally got to work, my boss was in a meeting and didn't even notice I was late.

Suddenly, I realized I had left my phone at home. I couldn't believe it. I ran into my colleague Sara in the hallway and she lent me her phone for the day. In the end, the day turned out to be okay — but I definitely need a better alarm clock!

Perguntas de interpretação

1. Por que o narrador acordou tarde?

2. O que aconteceu quando ele chegou ao ponto de ônibus?

3. O que ele percebeu quando chegou ao trabalho?

4. Como o dia terminou no final?

Foco na língua

→ Encontre todos os conectores de sequência usados no texto.

→ Encontre a expressão "to make matters worse" e explique o que aconteceu.

→ Encontre o phrasal verb "run into" no texto.
