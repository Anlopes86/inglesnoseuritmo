Comparing places

Use comparative adjectives to compare cities, countries and places.

Nesta aula você aprende a usar adjetivos comparativos para comparar lugares — cidades, países, bairros — de forma natural e precisa.

By the end, you can:

  • formar comparativos com -er (shorter) e com more (more expensive);
  • usar than corretamente na comparação;
  • usar comparativos irregulares: good → better, bad → worse.

Opening Dialogue — Choosing a holiday destination

Paula:

"I'm thinking about going to Lisbon or Porto for my holiday. Which one do you prefer?"

James:

"Hmm, that's a tough one. Lisbon is bigger than Porto, but Porto is more charming in my opinion."

Paula:

"What about the food? Is Porto better than Lisbon for restaurants?"

James:

"Both are amazing, but Porto is cheaper than Lisbon. Hotels are more affordable too."

Paula:

"Really? And is it farther to get there?"

James:

"Not really. The train is faster than the bus and more comfortable too. I think Porto is the better choice for you!"

Grammar Focus: Comparative Adjectives

Adjetivos curtos → + er

Adjetivos de 1 sílaba geralmente recebem -er no final.

talltaller
cheapcheaper
fastfaster
bigbigger (dobra a consoante)

Adjetivos longos → more +

Adjetivos de 2+ sílabas geralmente usam more antes.

expensivemore expensive
modernmore modern
crowdedmore crowded
peacefulmore peaceful

Irregulares — Memorize!

goodbetter
badworse
farfarther / further

Atenção: use "than"!

❌ "London is more expensive Paris."

✅ "London is more expensive than Paris."

Sempre use than quando estiver comparando duas coisas diretamente.

Adjetivos terminados em -y → troca por -ier

noisy → noisier lively → livelier friendly → friendlier busy → busier

Atividades: Comparative Adjectives

A — Complete com o comparativo correto

B — Coloque as palavras na ordem correta

C — Encontre e corrija o erro

Expressões & Phrasal Verbs

Expressões úteis para comparar e dar opiniões sobre lugares.

Phrasal Verb

Grow on you

Significado: algo que você não gostou de início, mas foi gostando com o tempo

"At first I didn't like the city, but it grew on me."

No começo não gostei da cidade, mas fui me apaixonando por ela.

Phrasal Verb

Stand out

Significado: se destacar, ser melhor ou diferente dos outros

"The architecture really stands out in this city."

A arquitetura realmente se destaca nessa cidade.

Expressão

There's no place like...

Significado: não existe lugar igual a... (expressa amor por um lugar)

"There's no place like home."

Não existe lugar igual ao lar.

Expressão

Worth a visit

Significado: vale a pena visitar

"Porto is definitely worth a visit!"

Porto definitivamente vale a pena visitar!

Mini Diálogos

Situação 1 — Escolhendo onde morar

A:

"Are you thinking about moving to the city centre?"

B:

"Maybe. It's more convenient, but it's also noisier and more expensive."

A:

"What about the suburbs? They're safer and cheaper."

B:

"True, but the commute is longer. It's a tough choice!"

Situação 2 — Recomendando um destino

A:

"I want to visit Portugal. Should I go to Lisbon or Porto?"

B:

"Porto is smaller but more charming. And it's cheaper than Lisbon."

A:

"Really? Is it worth a visit?"

B:

"Absolutely! The architecture really stands out. There's no place like it!"

Situação 3 — Comparando países

A:

"Which is better for a holiday — Japan or Thailand?"

B:

"Japan is more expensive, but the food is better. Thailand is warmer and more relaxed."

A:

"And which is farther from Brazil?"

B:

"Japan is a bit farther. Both are worth a visit, though!"

Situação 4 — Mudança de opinião

A:

"Did you like New York when you first arrived?"

B:

"Not really. It was noisier and more crowded than I expected."

A:

"And now?"

B:

"It grew on me! Now I think it's better than any other city I've lived in."

Leitura: City vs. Countryside

Um texto comparando dois estilos de vida usando os comparativos da aula.

Texto

Many people dream of moving to a big city, while others prefer the quiet life of the countryside. But which is really better? Let's compare.

Cities are generally more convenient than the countryside. Public transport is faster, shops are closer, and there are more job opportunities. However, city life is also more expensive. Rent is higher, food is pricier, and the streets are more crowded and noisier.

The countryside, on the other hand, is more peaceful and cleaner. The air is fresher and life is generally slower — which many people find healthier. Houses are bigger and cheaper than in the city. The community is often friendlier too.

In the end, it depends on your lifestyle. For young professionals, the city might be the better choice. For families, the countryside is often safer and more relaxed. There's no place like home — wherever that may be!

Perguntas de interpretação

1. Quais são duas vantagens de morar na cidade?

2. Por que a vida no campo é considerada mais saudável?

3. Para quem a cidade é a melhor escolha, segundo o texto?

4. Para quem o campo é mais indicado?

Foco na língua

→ Encontre 3 comparativos com -er no texto.

→ Encontre 3 comparativos com "more" no texto.

→ Encontre o comparativo irregular no texto.
