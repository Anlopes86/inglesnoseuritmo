Grammar Focus: as...as / too / enough

as...as — Igualdade

Use as + adjective + as para dizer que duas coisas são iguais. Para negar, use not as...as.

She is as fast as her brother.

Ela é tão rápida quanto o irmão.

This hotel is not as expensive as that one.

Este hotel não é tão caro quanto aquele.

Is he as strong as you?

Ele é tão forte quanto você?

This city is as noisy as New York.

Esta cidade é tão barulhenta quanto Nova York.

too — Excesso

Use too + adjective para dizer que algo é mais do que o desejado ou necessário. Tem sentido negativo.

This bag is too heavy.

Esta bolsa é pesada demais.

The music is too loud for me.

A música está alta demais para mim.

enough — Suficiência

Use adjective + enough para dizer que algo tem a quantidade certa. Para negar, use not + adjective + enough.

She is confident enough to lead the team.

Ela é confiante o suficiente para liderar.

He is not fast enough to win.

Ele não é rápido o suficiente para ganhar.

⚠️ Atenção: posição de enough

enough sempre vem depois do adjetivo: fast enough ✅ — nunca antes: enough fast ❌

Expressions & Phrasal Verbs

Expressões naturais para comparar e avaliar situações.

Expression

Keep up with

Significado: acompanhar, manter o ritmo de algo ou alguém

"She runs as fast as me — I can barely keep up with her."

Ela corre tão rápido quanto eu — mal consigo acompanhá-la.

Phrasal Verb

Give up

Significado: desistir, parar de tentar

"Don't give up — you are good enough to succeed."

Não desista — você é bom o suficiente para ter sucesso.

Expression

Way too + adjective

Significado: muito mais do que o necessário (intensificador de "too")

"This coffee is way too strong for me."

Este café é forte demais para mim. (muito mais do que deveria)

Expression

More than enough

Significado: mais do que suficiente, de sobra

"We have more than enough food for everyone."

Temos comida mais do que suficiente para todos.

Mini Dialogues

Situation 1 — At the gym

A:

"Can you keep up with the instructor?"

"Not really. She's way too fast for me!"

A:

"Don't give up. You'll be as fast as her soon."

Situation 2 — Choosing a restaurant

A:

"Is this restaurant good enough for a birthday dinner?"

B:

"It's more than enough! The food is amazing."

Situation 3 — At work

A:

"Do you think I'm experienced enough for this job?"

B:

"Absolutely. You're as skilled as anyone on the team."

Situation 4 — Planning a trip

A:

"The hotel is way too expensive. Let's find another one."

B:

"Good idea. This one is not as comfortable anyway."

Reading: The New Employee

Leitura guiada com as...as, too, enough e vocabulário de qualidades profissionais.

Text

Marcus just started his new job at a tech company. On his first day, he felt the office was way too quiet — he was used to working in noisy places. His new colleagues were as friendly as the people at his old job, but the work was more demanding.

His manager, Sarah, told him: "You are skilled enough to handle this project. Just don't give up when things get difficult." Marcus tried to keep up with the team's pace, but the deadlines were too tight for him at first.

After two weeks, Marcus felt more confident. The team had more than enough resources to finish the project, and Marcus was working as efficiently as his colleagues. He realized that the job was not too hard — it was just new.

Comprehension Questions

1. Why did Marcus find the office too quiet?

2. What did his manager tell him about giving up?

3. How did Marcus feel after two weeks?

4. Find one example of "as...as" in the last paragraph.
